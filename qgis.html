Interactive Recruiting Map

Explore different recruiting layers: player locations, school footprints, travel zones, and more.

This map visualizes the geographic distribution of college football recruits for each school, using standard deviation ellipses to represent the spatial spread of recruited players. Each ellipse captures the core recruiting footprint for a program, showing the general area where most of their recruits are concentrated. Mean center points mark the average geographic location of recruits for each school, offering a quick visual cue to see where a program’s recruiting base is centered. Together, these tools reveal differences in recruiting strategies—some teams focus heavily on their local region, while others cast a wider national net.

This hub-and-spoke map visualizes the connections between player recruit hometowns and the schools they signed with, illustrating the geographic reach and recruitment pipelines of each program. Each player is represented by a point at their hometown, with lines extending to their chosen program, revealing patterns of regional dominance, cross-country and international recruiting tendencies, and outlier signings. This map highlights the varying geographic strategies programs use to secure talent.

This map visualizes player recruiting hotspots using a heatmap to identify regions with the highest concentration of college football recruits. Warmer colors indicate areas with a dense supply of talent, while cooler areas represent regions with fewer recruits. The visualization highlights well-established recruiting powerhouses like the Southeast, Texas, and California, while also revealing smaller, less traditional hotspots in areas like the Northeast corridor, Seattle, and Detroit—patterns likely driven, in part, by the presence of nearby College Football Playoff contenders from this season.

1-Hour Drive-Time Isochrones by Team

These static images were created in QGIS to visualize 1-hour drive-time isochrones around each program. Each dot marks the hometown of players signed by the school featured on the map. A single dot may represent multiple players from the same town. The map is school-specific and does not display recruits from other programs in the area. To explore the underlying data and interact with the maps directly, click any image to view the live version on ArcGIS Online.
