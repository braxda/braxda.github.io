Tableau Dashboards

Explore my interactive dashboards created for each playoff team, designed to showcase key insights in their recruiting trends and player distributions. These dashboards provide an in-depth look at the patterns and strategies behind team rosters, offering a visual representation of how these teams were composed. You'll notice disparities in the average talent make up of each team, as well as see differences in strategies in terms of local versus long-distance recruiting.

Michigan Dashboard

Michigan maintains a relatively low average star rating for recruits, and a somewhat even distance distribution. Michigan also holds the lowest In-State Percentage of any of the 4 schools. This information can give us some insight on Michigan's strategies. Since the local Michigan region does not produce much high-end talent, the university is forced to spread it's recruiting resources thinner over a wider area. You'll also notice Michigan's propensity to target highly rated offensive linemen and running backs, which reflected in their National Championship victory, where the team combined for over 300 rushing yards and 4 rushing touchdowns.

Washington Dashboard

Washington has the lowest average star rating of any of the 4 schools, and only one 5 star player. Like Michigan, they also have a wide recruiting range, again due to a lack of concentration of high-end in-state talent. Washington does benefit from the talent-rich state of California, as most of their recruits are concentrated on the west coast. A majority of Washington's blue chip talent is focused on the offensive side of the ball. This played out as the team was hoping, as they finished second in the entire country in passing yards in 2023.

Texas Dashboard

A staggering 73% of Texas' players come from in-state, dwarfing second place Alabama at a relatively measly 42%. This highlights that the Dallas-Fort Worth Metro Area's is perhaps the premier college football talent hub, in fact, each of the 4 schools all had at least 3 players from the DFW region. The in-state players Texas did recruit were also very highly regarded, as the team carries the highest average player star rating, and an impressive 46% of players were blue-chip prospects. With such a talent-rich area, the University of Texas is perfectly positioned to capitalize on its prime location.

Alabama Dashboard

Alabama's recruiting strategy, like Texas, is also highly-regionalized, with a majority of recruits coming from the Southeast United States. This is also a very talent-dense region, with metros such as Birmingham, Atlanta, and Miami feeding highly-regarded players to the university. Over half the team is composed of blue chip players, which highlights Alabama's huge influence over this hotbed region of the country.
